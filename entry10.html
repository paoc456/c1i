70 7th Ave S, New York, NY 10014

Although it is now permanently closed, this restaurant signage is one of the first few that featured a simplistic and elegant mix of latin letters and a Chinese/Korean character. This thicker, slab serif paired well with the uniform lineweight of the character.

251 Bleecker St, New York, NY 10014

What appeared to be a design studio that no longer existed, this monochromatic tile in an art-deco-esque typeface caught my eye from across the street. I liked how rounded yet sophisticated the letterforms appeared, and the texture it created due to its three dimensional form.

4 Morton St, New York, NY 10014

An art gallery space with a monogram logo/sign displayed on its door. The zig-zag, lightning bolt design of the 'm' was an interesting play on the street name, set in a thick, black typeface in a striking yellow tone.

298 Bleecker St, New York, NY 10014

As a complete stickler for chunky sans-serifs, this one was extremely satisfying to look at, particularly with the addition of a drop shadow. I appreciated that the dot of the 'i' was in line with the cap-height, and the attention given to the negative spaces of the letterforms.

540 Hudson St, New York, NY 10014

This typeface would not be one I immediately associate with ice cream, but the bold red colour of the ampersand was what drew me in. Its exaggerated size paired next to the other embellished letters of the shop work nicely together as a unique brand identity.

550 Hudson St, New York, NY 10014

Inside of this cafe was an array of merchandise and caffeine products. I immediately noticed the label of this matcha can with what I think is a handlettering typeface paired with a delicate sans-serif font. The custom, handwritten style feels effective and personalised.
