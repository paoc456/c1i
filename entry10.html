Flushing

Entry 10

Flushing is a neighbourhood in the Northern part of Queens. Historically, it was occupied by the Matinecock
Indian tribe, a division of the Algonquin Nation. It is now home to a large immigrant population, with one of
the largest Chinatowns outside of Asia.



38-1 Prince St, Flushing, NY 11354

I was greeted by this map and signage near a bakery I had visited. This was one of the first instances where I had found vertically typeset lettters and all capitals. I particularly liked the natural drop shadow that formed due to the raised surface.

135-15 37th Ave, Flushing, NY 11354

This was an interesting banner logo, as the light strip signs and Chinese characters overlap with the pink, sans-serif type (what I assume to be Helvetica). However, I liked this interplay of a rounded Chinese script with a flatter latin font.

135-32 37th Ave, Flushing, NY 11354

I felt that this was an intriguing mix of Comic Sans and a handwritten typeface, with over exaggerated round strokes but not quite wobbly enough to be Comic Sans. The 'a' in particular is very circular with a slight tail.

37-18 Main St, Flushing, NY 11354

By far one of the most free-handed typefaces I've seen, the individual characters are all uniquely different in size and shape, with varied heavy and light strokes. I thought the tail stroke on the 'R' extending was a clever choice.

4144 Kissena Blvd, Flushing, NY 11355

This signage was interesting in that the logic and style for both the latin and Chinese characters translated across one another with conjoined strokes and squared-off bowls despite being very rounded overall.

41-28 Main St, Flushing, NY 11354

Common with a lot of the other restaurant and store logos I saw in Flushing, this logo replaces a letter with a graphic illustration. It has a mix of rounded and straight strokes, with generally condensed letterforms.
