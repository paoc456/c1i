97 Montrose Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11206
Robert E. Smith's Brush Script spotted within restaurant signage. One of my less favoured fonts but appears often for eateries and 'retro' logo designs,
at least it's a nice shade of cornflower blue. Its slight tilt caught my attention, appearing handwritten and personalised.

163 Montrose Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11206
An enticing use of small cap letters. The thick, blocky, three-dimensional type in beige and bright pink paired not so well with the script font (or so I thought),
but had a unique flair that suited the logo illustration.

Unknown address, Brooklyn
Not formally considered a typeface, but something that always catches my eye in New York. I did indeed stop and look.
There is a certain emphemerality to this type of writing (or vandalism), disappearing when construction is complete.

254 S 2nd St, Brooklyn, NY 11211
I previously only focused on their pizza, but going back this time, it really forced my eye to see their signage and branding.
A satisfying brush script type with a slant that compliments each individual letter. The wordspacing was distinct, not a detail I've often see with script fonts.
