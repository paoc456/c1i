East Village

Entry 06

A collection of signage seen during my stroll in the East Village.
The East Village is nested between East 4th and East 14th Street, Bowery and Avenue A.
The area is known to be artsy, home to many eateries, night life, and boutique stores.
The area was home to a large community of Ukrainian, Jewish, and Puerto Rican immigrants.



35 Cooper Sq, New York, NY 10003

A font that I instantly recognised as soon as I walked past - Misto, a funky all-caps typeface with unique glyphs and a blend of thick and thin lines. I particularly liked the way the dot extended past the cap height of all letters.

207 2nd Ave, New York, NY 10003

What caught my eye initially was the text warp within the logo. Then, I noticed the notch on the 'J' and 'A', adding visual interest to the logo. I found the slight curve/tail of these letter forms to be most intriguing.

223 E 5th St, New York, NY 10003

A groovy, all-lowercase typeface and logo for a Japanese handroll bar. Although not my preferred colour palette, I admired the way the stems and bowls of the 'a' and 'k' curved, the change in stroke weight feels satisfying to the eye.

181-189 2nd Ave, New York, NY 10003

The marquee outside of a movie theatre is a form of graphic design that I never really paid attention to. The typography is always interchangable and exists three dimensionally in its sans-serif form.

25 Cooper Sq, New York, NY 10003

One of my favourite discoveries thus far. This was a sign on a gate outside a hotel, with the greenery surrounding it making its design all the more beautiful. I loved the negative spaces created within the letters and warping of the letters.
