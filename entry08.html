Beacon

Entry 08

A series of signage observed throughout the city of Beacon, primarily on its Main Street.
It is 60 miles North of New York City and was one of the first communities of the state.
It was known as the 'Hat Making Capital' of the United States, functioning as a factory town
until 1970 when it saw an economic decline.



297 Main St, Beacon, NY 12508

A curved, symmetrical typeface design specifically for the restaurant Enoteca Ama. The rounded stroke on the letter 'A' was the most intriguing to me, the letter forms felt reminiscent of art deco styles but still suited its cuisine.

285 Main St, Beacon, NY 12508

An interesting mix of condensed typefaces in a retro, painted signage style. The use of all capital letters drew my attention in a typeface I most associate with American media, and one of the first instances of fonts using drop shadows.

209 Main St, Beacon, NY 125085

Unlike other typefaces I've seen thus far, this was on the ground and made of tile rather than one solid unit. It was the entrance to a hair salon, with hexagonal tiles forming these distinct numbers greeting you at the door.

199 Main St, Beacon, NY 12508

One of my favourite typefaces of all time, Futura, spotted on packaging design inside of a home goods store. Its distinct, sharp letterforms are immediately recognisable and is used effectively in conjunction with the duotoned palette.

188 Main St, Beacon, NY 12508

Nestled within a collection of old books within an antique store, was a book cover with embellished serif letters. I particularly appreciated the art nouveau styled design and slanted serifs of the title, a dynamic design that I don't see often.

Main St, Beacon, NY 12508

What I believe to be Helvetica, was displayed on the main sign at the entrance. The hierarchy of the signage was executed well and I liked the mix of capital letters with lowercase, particularly in this bolder font weight.
