Fort Greene

Entry 07

Tyepfaces seen throughout the neighbourhood of Fort Greene in Brooklyn.
Fort Greene was named after the Fort Greene Park, which was a critical landmark during
the American Revolution, with the name taking after Nathanael Greene, a war general.
The district is full of brownstone buildings, nature and many community activities.



757 Fulton St, Brooklyn, NY 11217

As soon as I came out from the subway exit, I was greeted with a lime green restaurant banner. From what I could tell about the quaint eatery, this looked to be a hand-painted sign reminiscent of retro-styled brush script.

96 Lafayette Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11217

An aspect of type I don't often notice, which are numbers. This was a set of number tiles in a style I had never come across before, its porcelain quality and cobalt blue glaze made it stand out against the other brownstones next to it.

323-321 Clermont Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11205

A random red truck I came across while walking to my next destination. This black style typeface on the back of the truck, with a three dimensional drop shadow embellishment was extremely eyecatching.

279 Lafayette Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11238

Back on Lafayette Avenue, I spotted a gothic style typeface on the front of a church entrance. These individual letters are the rounded, uncial styled characters that date back to historical developments of typography.

448 Lafayette Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11205

A distinct, blocky typeface to compliment a donut shop brand identity. What caught my eye the most was the outline version of the typeface, how the strokes of the 'K' in particular are separate shapes.

471 Dekalb Ave, Brooklyn, NY 11205

A picturesque eatery sign that I noticed near the Pratt campus. The serif typeface was quite delicate and elegant, and I thought it was visually interesting how its letterspacing is played with due to the panels of the glass/wood.
